Safety First

Safety is my priority and takes precedence over any other consideration, my mission is to operate at the highest standards and comply with all regulations and obligations laid down by Transport Canada and my operations manual.
Thorough planning goes into every flight, including a pre-deployment survey, on-site survey, checking NOTAM's, sensitive locations and risk assessments.
Due to this I can proudly say I've completed over 100 professional flights without incident.

About me

From a young age I have been fascinated with technology, R/C cars and model planes were a big part of my childhood and similar hobby's continue to this day. In my spare time you will find me building with arduino’s, coding or spending hours tweaking settings on my 3D printer. It was with no surprise then as drones grew in popularity I was quick to join the action. Since 2014 I’ve been flying drones, for the first couple of years it was only a hobby as I built micro drones for indoor flying and faster 250 size drones for racing outdoors.
From there I moved onto building more advanced and larger autonomous platforms for professional use.

Recently

As a CAA approved drone pilot I have spent the last two years working in the U.K as Chief Pilot for Tomorrow's Forests. Our main focus was investigating how drones could be implemented into the forestry and ecology industries to improve current practices such as stock density analysis, canopy closure, pest/disease tracking and wind-blow inspection among others. Along side offering aerial photo and videography services.
I have since returned to Canada and look forward to continuing work in the industry.
